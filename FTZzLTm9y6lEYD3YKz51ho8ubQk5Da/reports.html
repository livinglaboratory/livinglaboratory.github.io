Creating a Living Laboratory for Deadmans Head Forest During the Age of Climate Change

Deadmans Head Forest in Blacks Harbor, Southwestern New Brunswick, is a New England Acadian Forest nestled on rolling terrain by the Bay of Fundy. With a blend of coniferous and deciduous trees, it supports diverse wildlife. Its eastern side boasts cliff beaches, while sandy beaches line the western edge.

Intertidal Zones within Deadmans Harbour and the Bay of Fundy

Ernie and Judy Edwards, owners of Deadmans Head Peninsula, are establishing a living laboratory to study climate change's impact on the coastal ecosystem. Positioned in the Bay of Fundy, known for its highest tides globally, Phase One evaluated the forested landscape's flora and fauna, while Phase Two focuses on the intertidal zones' community structure.
