About

What is happening with all the other species now that we are in the Anthropocene era? How will these species react and adapt to our ever growing negative impact on their lives? This is what I want to identify, study and hopefully provide help to.

The initial study area is a 100+ acre, 2 1/2 miles of ocean-front shoreline peninsula that juts out into the Bay of Fundy. To commence the development of a “living laboratory” on this site, a group of four University of New Brunswick graduate students studied possible approaches for establishing a laboratory of this kind.

Learn about Creating a Living Laboratory

Goals

Identify and detail current species on and around this site using iNaturalist.org

Track over time how these species respond to the climate.

As much as possible use remote tools to minimize human engagement.

Share data with other like minded organizations and groups.

Encourage and work with expanding other research sites.
